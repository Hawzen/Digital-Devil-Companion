Introduction

Welcome to the Demon Compendium, a guide on the demons found in Shin Megami Tensei: Devil Surivor Overclocked. Much of the information on game mechanics contained in this guide has been researched by me, and to my knowledge (as of the time of writing this) cannot be found anywhere else. As it has been determined empirically, and not by peeking at the code, it may not be exactly how the game works; it should, however, be an accurate enough description of what is occuring for you to manipulate the outcome.

My aim in this guide is to help you get what you want out of your demons. Do you just want to find out where to get certain abilities? The Demon Info has you covered. Do you want to manipulate your fusions to make the most of your demons for a challenge? Understanding the game mechanics will help you get there. Do you want to take your demons to the limits and stomp all over the bonus battle? We can do that. Hopefully you'll find everything you want within this guide.

Obtaining Demons

Outside of the first mission, demons can be obtained in three ways:

  1. Purchased from the Auction House
  2. Created by fusing two demons together
  3. Purchase a previously registered demon from the compendium

The Auction House

Once a demon has been beaten in battle, it will show up in the Auction House. However, only certain demons show up in the auction house, and only if the appropriate rank has been purchased. Each rank beyond Basic has both a Rating requirement and a Day requirement; only if both conditions are met will the option to purchase that rank be presented.

Within all ranks, the Raise - the amount each bid is required to be over the previous - is constant. The Raise is 1 for Basic, 10 for Gold, and 100 for both Platinum and Occult.

Fusing Demons

Two demons can be fused together to produce a third, new demon. This demon is determined by the level and race of the two parent demons. In this way, one can obtain powerful demons that one would not be able to purchase from the Auction House. One can also choose skills from the parents to give to the newly created demon, allowing a level of customization not offered by the Auction House.

To determine the newly created demon's race, take the races of both parents and consult the chart below. The specific demon is then chosen from the next available demon of that race thats level is greater than or equal to the average of the base levels of the parents.

Note that some demons can only be created by special fusions, which don't follow the normal rules of fusion. Shiva, for instance, can only be created by fusing a Barong (Avatar) with a Rangda (Femme) even though Shiva is a Deity and not a Genma.

Another thing to keep in mind is that some fusions must be unlocked. See Unlockable Demons below for further details. Fusions that would produce a locked demon will instead not work.

Using Elements

As an alternative to messing with around with fusion requirements, fusing with an Elemental can be a quick and simple way to obtain a desired demon.

Elements are created by fusing two demons of the same race and paying an additional charge of 1,000 Macca. Combat-wise, Elements are of little value: they don't have particularly impressive stats, they're slightly less customizable than other demons, and they don't have a racial ability. However, when fused with another demon, they can cause it to rank up or rank down within its race. In other words, the race stays the same, but the particular demon is either the next highest or lowest level demon within that race. They can also be used to create Mitamas; see Using Mitamas below.

The particular Element created depends on the race used and the individual elements affect the races differently. Some of the more desirable races, such as Megami and Tyrant, aren't ranked up by any of the elements; if one wants to upgrade them, it is necessary to use normal fusion.

The Compendium

Newly added in Overclocked, the Demon Compendium is unlocked in the menu on the third day. Once obtained, one can purchase copies of previously owned demons for a price; the price is determined based on their level, stats, and abilities. However, only a single version of each demon obtained is saved in the compendium. When first obtained, they are automatically registered, but any changes made must be manually saved using the register option.

Demon Mechanics

Determining HP and MP

A demon's HP and MP are determined by the demon's Level and Vitality (for HP) and Level and Magic (for MP.) The individuality comes from two hidden stats: HP Growth and MP Growth. Every demon has it own values for these stats, and they cannot be changed. To determine what a demon's HP or MP would be, find their respective growth (see the demon section below) and plug their stats into one of the following formulae:

HP = (Level + Vitality) * HPGrowth

MP = (Level + Magic) * MPGrowth

Note that the total cannot exceed 999.

Three Stats: Base, Bonus and Level

A demon's stats can be divided into three groups: Base Stats, Bonus Stats, and Level Stats.

Base Stats

These are the stats a demon has at its lowest level. On the demon's summary screen, they show up as a dark blue bar. A demon's stats cannot be lowered beyond its Base Stats by normal means. (See Using Penalties to Lower Stats for further detail.)

Bonus Stats

Bonus Stats are how one customizes a demon. They can be obtained in two ways: either via fusion bonuses for a new demon, or by fusing an existing demon with a Mitama. They show up on the stat screen as a light blue portion of the stat bar and, unlike the other two, they can be shifted around. Bonus stats also provide a bonus towards newly fused demons.

The amount of bonus stats a demon can have is determined by the following formula, with the max being 24 at Level 99:

Bonus Stats = 5 + (Level/5)

Level Stats

These are the stats a demon would earn naturally as it levels up. They behave similarly to both Base Stats and Bonus stats; like Bonus Stats, they show up as a light blue on a demons stat screen, and they also provide bonuses to fusion. However, like Base Stats, they cannot be lowered by normal means.

If, through the use of Bonus Stats, a demon hits the cap on a stat early, Bonus Stats can be converted to Level Stats.

Leveling Up and Growth Patterns

For every level a demon earns, they gain a single point in one stat. At first it may seem that these gains are random, but in actuality, they are all preset. Every Pixie will level up identically, and the stats they gain are different from an Ogre. This means one does not have to worry about getting RNG screwed, but it also means that the role a demon can perform is fairly fixed.

The Growth Pattern of a demon follows a cycle of 16 level ups, except for Fairy Vivian, which has a cycle of 15. These stats can be subdivided into two groups: Specialization and Generic. Specialization periods can range from 4 (the most common) to 12 stats, and focus on certain stats while leaving others out. Generic periods give the demon a boost to all stats, in the order of Strength, Magic, Vitality, and Agility. All demons have at least one Generic period, though most have three.

All demons start their growth on the second term of their Growth Pattern. In other words, their first level up will result in them gaining the second stat listed.

As an example, here is a Pixie's Growth Pattern:

Ma, Ma, Ma, Vi // St, Ma, Vi, Ag // St, Ma, Vi, Ag // St, Ma, Vi, Ag

The first four level ups a Pixie gains will be, in order, Magic, Magic, Vitality, and Strength. This is because it starts on the second term of the Growth Pattern, not the first.

When a demon hits 40 in a stat - the highest a stat can go - any further gains in that stat are instead replaced with the next available term. This does not effect the position in the Growth Pattern, however.

For instance, if a Pixie has managed to max Vitality, its growth would be as follows:

Ma, Ma, Ma, St // St, Ma, Ag, Ag // St, Ma, Ag, Ag // St, Ma, Ag, Ag

Using Mitamas

When two different Elements are fused, the product is a Mitama. Like Elements, Mitamas come with an additional charge; in this case, 3,000 Macca. Combat-wise, Mitamas are similar to Elements in that they serve little use. Instead, like Elements, Mitamas are meant to be used in fusion. When fused with a demon, they give it up to 3 Bonus Stats, as well as allow the transfer of abilities. The particular stat boosted depends on the Mitama: Ara Mitamas boost Strength, Nigi Mitamas boost Magic, Saki Mitamas boost Vitality, and Kusi Mitamas boost Agility.

The stat boost is limited by the normal stat cap of 40, as well as by the amount of Bonus Stats the demon may have at its level. If the +3 would cause a demon to exceed the stat cap, it only gains the difference between its current stat and 40. If the +3 causes the demon to exceed its allowed Bonus Stats, if possible, the difference will be removed from the Bonus Stats the demon has in one of the other stats, selected randomly. If the demon does not have bonus points in any other stats, it will instead gain only as many points as its limit allows.

Rules of Fusion Bonuses

Mitamas, while the simplest, are not the only way for a demon to gain Bonus Stats. Newly fused demons also get Bonus Stats, and unlike with Mitamas, there is no additional charge. Through careful manipulation, one can fuse powerful demons for cheap.

The rules for Fusion Bonus are as such:

  • Bonus stats are determined by the parents.
  • All demons have at least one Favored Stat. When used as an ingredient in fusion, for the purpose of calculating the Bonus they get a +1 to the Favored Stat with the highest value. If there is a tie for the highest value, all tied stats get the bonus. See "Determining Favored Stats" below for further details.
  • Level Stats transfer at a rate of 50%.
  • Bonus Stats transfer at a rate of 80%.
  • Like all numbers in the game, Level Stats and Bonus Stats are truncated after the decimal immediately after being calculated and prior to being added together.
  • The new demon's Bonus in each stat is the sum of the parents transferred values.
  • Stats still cap at 40. Anything extra is lost immediately.
  • After all Bonuses for the new demon are calculated, the last point of one of the greater-than-zero Bonuses is removed and randomly assigned to one of the four stats.
  • If the new demon's Bonuses would cause it to exceed its allowed Bonus Stats, the actual distribution is randomly selected from within the possible amounts of the inherited Bonuses. The sum of all Bonuses equals the demon's allowed Bonus.

Mathematically, the bonus for a given stat can be expressed by the following formula:

New Demon's Bonus = (L1+F1)*0.5 + (B1+F1)*0.8 + (L2+F2)*0.5 + (B2+F2)*0.8

L = Level-up points earned in the given stat
B = Bonus points earned in the given stat
F = Favored; 1 if yes, 0 if no.
1 & 2 = Parent one and two, respectively

Example 1: Macabre has gained 8 levels and been fused with one Ara Mitama, giving it level-up stats of +3 St, +1 Ma, +2 Vi, +2 Ag and bonus stats of +3 St.
Catoblepas hasn't been leveled at all, but it has been fused with one of each Mitama, giving it bonus stats of +3 St, +3 Ma, +3 Vi, +3 Ag.
Both Catoblepas and Macabre have a Favored Stat of Strength.

Determing Favored Stats

Note: This section is academic and is not necessary for manipulating demons. All demons' Favored Stats can be found in their information below.

A demon's Favored Stat or Stats are determined by two things:

  1. Their Growth Pattern.
  2. Their current stats.

The most important part is their Growth Pattern, as it determines whether a stat can be favored or not. In order for a stat to be a Favored Stat, it has to have the highest gain in the 16-level cycle. In other words, whichever stat shows up the most in their Growth Pattern is the Favored Stat.

If two or more stats are tied for most frequent stat in the Growth Pattern, which one is favored is determined by the demon's current stats; whichever stat currently has a higher value will gain the Favored Stat bonus during fusion. In the event of a tie in current stats as well, all tied stats get the +1 bonus.

Example: Shiva has a growth pattern of: St, Vi, St, Vi // St, Ma, Vi, Ag // St, Ma, Vi, Ag // St, Ma, Vi, Ag

Strength and Vitality are tied for most frequently occuring stat, at 5 each per cycle. Therefore, both Strength and Vitality are elligible to get the Favored Stat bonus. Magic and Agility each only occur 3 times per cycle and are thus inelligible.

At level 61, after being fused with an Ara Mitama and a Saki Mitama, Shiva has the following stats: 26 St, 20 Ma, 26 Vi, 18 Ag. Since both Strength and Vitality are currently equal, if Shiva was used in Fusion, it would give a +1 to both stats.

After gaining a level, Shiva gains a single point of Vitality, making its stats: 26 St, 20 Ma, 27 Vi, 18 Ag. Since its Vitality is now higher than its Strength, only Vitality will get a bonus if the Shiva is used in fusion.

Using Penalties to Lower Stats

Warning: The following was most likely not intended by the developers.

In the Auction House, there are various 1 and 2-star demons with a portion of their stat bar colored red. These are stat penalties, a reduction of their stats beyond their base stats. However, they are not a fourth stat type, and are instead treated like Bonus Stats with a negative value.

So, what does this mean? Well, it means they follow all of the rules associated with Bonus Stats. This, in turn, means that, through careful manipulation, they can be distributed to other demons, allowing the reduction of demons stats to levels not normally possible. It also means that there are more Bonus Stats to distribute. The game looks at a Bonus of (+24, -1) as +23, meaning there is one more point to distribute.

Unfortunately, because the penalties follow all the rules associated with Bonus Stats, Magic cannot be easily reduced, and Strength cannot be reduced at all. This is because the Favored Stat bonus prevents placing either of them on a Mitama. Vitality and Agility work, at least.

Creating a Mitama to reduce Vitality

  1. Choose either Ara Mitama (+St) or Kusi Mitama (+Ag.) This is the Mitama to which you will be giving the negative modifier. If you wish to use a Nigi Mitama (+Ma), you must first follow these steps before proceeding to the Going Beyond section below.
  2. Depending on your goal, you will either need to obtain two 1-star Fafnir (for Ara Mitama) or two 1-star Jahi (for Kusi Mitama.) In both cases, you will also need to obtain two 1-star Jambavan. Fafnir and Jahi are both found in the Occult rank auction, while Jambavan is found in the Basic rank. To obtain them, check the auction, purchase whichever are available, then save your game. If you still need more, soft reset (L + R + Start) and reload your file. You may wish to register them to your Compendium to save time in obtaining a second copy by allowing their immediate repurchase.
  3. Once you have the necessary demons, you need to convert them into Elements. The two Jamabavan create Aeros, while Fafnir makes Flaemis and Jahi makes Aquans. Your newly created Elements should have a penalty of -2 in Vitality. If they do not, you likely accidentally used the wrong rank materials.
  4. (Optional) Register your newly created Elements in the Compendium. It is highly advisable to register the Elements, instead of the Mitama, as it is much cheaper to repurchase and fuse two Elements than it is to repurchase a single Mitama.
  5. Fuse the two Elements to produce a Mitama. When used, this Mitama will provide a bonus of +3 to either Strength or Agility, and a bonus of -1 to Vitality.

Creating a Mitama to reduce Agility

  1. Choose either Ara Mitama (+St) or Saki Mitama (+Vi.) This is the Mitama to which you will be giving the negative modifier. It is possible to produce a Nigi Mitama (+Ma), but it's so convoluted that it's easier to fuse one of the other two and use the information in the Going Beyond section below to make a Nigi Mitama.
  2. If you are trying to make an Ara Mitama, proceed to step 2a. If you are trying to make a Kusi Mitama, proceed to step 2b.
    1. You will need four 1-star Spriggan, found in the Occult Rank auction, and two 1-star Badb Catha, found in the Platinum Rank auction. You will also need an additional two of either of them. It is highly advisable to register these to your compendium so as to save time and money.
    2. You will need six 1-star Spriggan, found in the Occult Rank auction, and four 1-star Badb Catha, found in the Platinum Rank auction. It is highly advisable to register these to your compendium so as to save time and money.
  3. If you are trying to make an Ara Mitama, proceed to step 3a. If you are trying to make a Kusi Mitama, proceed to step 3b.
    1. Combine two Spriggan with two Badb Catha to make two Seiryuu. Make sure these Seiryuu should have a penalty of -2 to their agility. If not, return to step 2a.
    2. Combine four Spriggan with four Badb Catha to make four Seiryuu. Make sure these Seiryuu have a penalty of -2 to their agility. After making the Seiryuu, combine two of them with another two Spriggan, creating two Cerberus. Once again, make sure these Cerberus have a penalty of -2 to their agility.
  4. Combine the demons you created in step 3, as well as any left over from step 2, to produce Elements. Badb Catha x2 produces Aeros, Spriggan x2 produces Aeros, Seiryuu x2 produces Aquans, and Cerberus x2 produces Flaemis. Make sure your Elements still have a -2 agility penalty.
  5. (Optional) Register your newly created Elements in the Compendium. It is highly advisable to register the Elements, instead of the Mitama, as it is much cheaper to repurchase and fuse two Elements than it is to repurchase a single Mitama.
  6. Fuse the two Elements to produce a Mitama. When used, this Mitama provides a bonus of +3 to either Strength or Vitality, and a bonus of -1 to Agility.

Going Beyond

Once an easily obtainable source of -1s is available from the compendium, there are a couple of options. One can either use them to produce Nigi Mitamas with a penalty, or fuse them with their original ingredients to produce Mitamas that reduce a stat by 2 or more.

When attempting to produce a Nigi Mitama, be mindful of Favored Stats. Element Erthys, for instance, has a Favored Stat of Vitality, which is why creating a Nigi Mitama with a Vitality penalty was initially not an option. However, with an easy, repeatable source of penalties, that +1 can be overcome by starting one of the ingredients with a -3.

When creating Mitamas that reduce a stat by 2 or more, remember that Stat Bonuses transfer at 80%, rounded towards zero. Since the penalty will be transferred three times (Ingredient -> Element -> Mitama -> Used), this means less than 50% of the initially distributed Bonus will make it to the target. The Elements also act as a limiting factor, as reducing their stat below zero will cause problems.

A 40 in Every Stat

Warning: The following was definitely not intended by the developers.

As an extra bit of fun, if a demon's stat is reduced beyond zero, it will loop around to an obscenely high number, causing the game to freak out momentarily. After exiting and re-entering the demons stat screen, the demons stat will have settled at a perfectly mundane 40.

This can, naturally, be exploited. However, before doing so, make sure all of the demon's passives are set up. After finishing this process, any further attempts to fuse the demon with a Mitama will cause its stats to be recalculated.

After all the demon's passives are set up, reduce the demon's Vitality or Agility to a low number; any single digit value should suffice. After that, use Mitamas to raise the rest of the demon's stats to 40. Finally, finish reducing the Vitality or Agility to below zero. Once the game recalculates the stat, it should be at 40, along with the rest of the demon's stats. Congratulations! You now have the oft dreamed of perfect demon!

Demons

Reading the Tables

  1. The name of the demon.
  2. The demon's race.
  3. If the demon is unique, it will be noted here. Players may only have one of each unique demon in their roster. Otherwise, the quantity of demons is not limited.
  4. The demons default stats at its starting level and level 99.
    1. Due to the way demons grow, penalties from the Auction House are not consistent across the levels; numbers in parenthesis indicate which stats can be reduced and by how much.
  5. The demon's possible Favored Stats. When used in fusion, the Favored Stat with the highest value will result in a small bonus in the corresponding stat of the newly created demon. See Rules of Fusion Bonuses for further details.
  6. The abilities of the demon.They are organized the same as in the game; the first three are Active, the next three are Passive, and the final is the demon's Racial.
    1. Abilities with a number in parenthesis indicate that the demon will learn that ability at the corresponding level.
    2. Abilities with AH # next to them are ones that can be obtained by purchasing the corresponding star-ranked demon on the Auction House.
  7. The amount of HP and MP the demon gains per level and point of Vitality or Magic. See Determining HP and MP for further details.
  8. The Base Stat Total (BST) of the demon. This is how many stat points above its level it has and serves as a way to compare demons.
  9. The demon's affinities. Only innate affinities (those listed in white in the game) are given. Affinities listed in yellow are the result of an ability the demon has, and are not included in this list. The abbreviations are: Wk - Weak; St - Strong; Nu - Null; Rf - Reflect; Dr - Drain
  10. The order in which the demon gains stats upon leveling. The first level up results in gaining the second term. See Leveling Up and Growth Patterns for further details.
  11. Changes made to the demon, if any, between the original Devil Survivor and Overclocked. The changes were determined by comparing to information provided by Cogito.
  12. What the demon says upon being created or being used in fusion.
  13. If the demon is available for purchase via the Auction House, further information can be found here.
  14. The starting bid of the demon.
  15. The buyout cost of the demon.
  16. The demon's stats at the given star-rank.
  17. How much the demon's stats differ from a completely generic version of the demon. Stats gained by leveling are not included in these totals.
